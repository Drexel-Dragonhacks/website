With workshops, mentorship, and resources for all skill levels, DragonHacks is your chance to turn ideas into reality.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is DragonHacks?
A 24-hour hackathon at Drexel for innovative projects.
Who can participate?
College students aged 18+ enrolled in any institution.
How will the hackathon be conducted?
DragonHacks XII will be an in-person event held at Drexel University, spanning 24 hours. It includes opening ceremonies, team formation, hacking sessions with workshops, and a final presentation phase with judging. All activities will take place in designated campus spaces with internet access and resources provided.
What are the dates for DragonHacks 2026?
DragonHacks XII is scheduled for January 2026. Exact dates will be announced closer to the event, with registration opening in October 2025.
Do I need to bring my own equipment?
Participants should bring their own laptops and chargers. Drexel will provide Wi-Fi, power strips, and basic hardware. Additional resources like Arduino kits or Raspberry Pis may be available on a first-come, first-served basis.
Are meals provided during the event?
Yes, meals, snacks, and beverages will be provided throughout the 24 hours, courtesy of our sponsors. Dietary restrictions can be accommodated if noted during registration.
How are teams judged?
Teams are judged based on code quality, innovation, and impact. A panel of judges will evaluate live demos and submissions on Devpost.
